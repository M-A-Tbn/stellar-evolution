# Post-MS: RGB, HB & AGB — Summary

## 1. Key Concepts

- **Point 2**: HRD point marking complete core H exhaustion; creates the core–envelope chemical dichotomy ($\mu_i \ne \mu_e$).
- **Chemical dichotomy**: He-rich core ($\mu_i=4/3$) beneath H-rich envelope ($\mu_e=0.63$); root cause of all post-MS structural evolution.
- **Point 3**: H-burning shell ignites ($u>0$); combined with chemical dichotomy, triggers envelope expansion.
- **Hertzsprung Gap**: Sparsely populated CMD region for rapid Segment 4–5 crossing (intermediate/high-mass stars); reflects short time spent there.
- **Sub-Giant Branch (SGB)**: Segment 4–5 becomes populated when crossing time is long (low-mass, old populations); its presence is an age indicator.
- **Hayashi Track (HT)**: Locus of fully convective models, nearly vertical at $T_\text{eff}\approx3500$–$4000$K; no stable hydrostatic models exist to its right.
- **Red Giant Branch (RGB)**: Ascent along/parallel to the HT, powered entirely by the H-shell; $L$ rises steeply while $T_\text{eff}$ changes little.
- **First Dredge-Up**: Convective envelope mixes CN-processed material to the surface; produces the CN anticorrelation and a frozen H-abundance discontinuity.
- **RGB Bump**: Temporary luminosity dip when the H-shell crosses the H-discontinuity from dredge-up; causes triple-crossing of a luminosity strip, a bump in the differential LF, and a slope change in the integrated LF.
- **Electron degeneracy**: Non-relativistic degenerate electrons give $P\propto\rho^{5/3}$, independent of $T$; removes thermoregulation.
- **Transition mass $M=2.2\,M_\odot$**: Boundary between degenerate (He-flash) and non-degenerate (stable) He ignition.
- **Helium Flash**: Thermonuclear runaway of $3\alpha$ in a degenerate He-core; energy consumed internally lifting degeneracy — no surface luminosity signature.
- **Off-center ignition**: Neutrino cooling creates a temperature inversion, so the Main Flash ignites off-center, followed by a cascade of sub-flashes working inward.
- **RGB Tip / TRGB**: Universal, mass-independent maximum RGB luminosity ($\log L/L_\odot\approx3.45$) for $M<2.2\,M_\odot$; used as a standard candle.
- **RGB Phase Transition**: Sudden appearance of a well-populated RGB when turnoff mass drops below $2.2\,M_\odot$ ($\sim$1–2 Gyr); integrated light reddens.
- **Reimers law**: Empirical RGB mass-loss prescription with free efficiency parameter $\eta$.
- **Zero Age Horizontal Branch (ZAHB)**: Locus of stable He-core ignition onset; analog of the ZAMS, positioned by $q=M_c/M_E$.
- **Second parameter problem**: Two clusters of equal age/metallicity can show very different HB morphology — an unresolved extra parameter (He abundance? mass loss?) is implicated.
- **EHB / AGB-manqué**: Extremely H-poor HB stars that skip the AGB, evolving directly to post-HB blue subdwarfs; linked to the UV upturn in old populations.
- **Overshooting**: Non-zero convective-cell velocity at the Schwarzschild border causes extra mixing beyond the formal boundary; parametrized by $\Lambda_\text{ov}$.
- **Semi-convection**: Marginally stable ($\nabla_\text{rad}\approx\nabla_\text{ad}$) partially-mixed zone forming a three-zone core structure during He-burning.
- **HB blue loop**: HRD loop from competing He-core expansion (segment A, bluer) and later core contraction/envelope expansion (segment B, redder).
- **Transition mass $M^\text{up}=8\,M_\odot$**: Boundary between degenerate CO-core (WD fate) and non-degenerate CO-core (Type II SN fate).
- **AGB (Asymptotic Giant Branch)**: Second ascent to the HT after He exhaustion in the core; double-shell burning phase.
- **Second Dredge-Up**: E-AGB mixing event bringing CN-processed material to the surface; significant only for $M>4$–$5\,M_\odot$.
- **Thermal Pulse (TP-AGB)**: Cyclic alternation of H-shell and He-shell burning, each pulse a minor thermonuclear runaway in the He-shell.
- **Third Dredge-Up**: Combination of intershell convection (pulse-driven) and envelope convection bringing C, O to the surface; source of Carbon Stars.
- **Carbon Star**: AGB star whose surface C/O > 1 after repeated Third Dredge-Up.
- **s-process**: Slow neutron capture (capture slower than $\beta$-decay) building elements past Fe along the valley of stability; AGB stars are a prime site.
- **Neutron poison**: $^{14}$N absorbs neutrons efficiently, suppressing the s-process unless removed.
- **Hot Bottom Burning (HBB)**: H-burning at the base of the convective envelope in massive AGB stars ($M>6$–$7\,M_\odot$); raises $L$, destroys surface C, produces Li via Cameron-Fowler.
- **Cameron-Fowler mechanism**: Convective transport of freshly made $^7$Li to cool layers before it is proton-destroyed, producing large Li overabundances.
- **NeNa / MgAl cycles**: High-T proton-capture cycles in HBB producing O-Na and Mg-Al anticorrelations seen in globular clusters.
- **AGB Phase Transition**: Onset of a well-populated, luminous AGB when turnoff mass drops below $8\,M_\odot$ ($\sim$200–300 Myr).

## 2. Key Equations

- $\mu = 1/(2X+\tfrac34 Y+\tfrac12 Z)$ — mean molecular weight from composition; gives $\mu_e=0.63$, $\mu_i=4/3$.
- $P=k\rho T/(\mu H)$ — ideal gas EoS; used to link $\rho,T,P$ across the core–envelope boundary.
- $\rho_i/\rho_e=\mu_i/\mu_e\approx2.11$ — density jump at the boundary from continuity of $P,T$.
- $\left(\frac{d\rho}{dr}\right)_e\Big/\left(\frac{d\rho}{dr}\right)_i=(\mu_e/\mu_i)^2\dfrac{\alpha-\gamma\beta}{\alpha-\beta}$ — master formula for the envelope/core density-gradient ratio; ratio $\ll1$ forces envelope expansion.
- $\gamma=(1+u)\dfrac{1+X_e}{1+X_i}\left(\dfrac{\mu_e}{\mu_i}\right)^n$ — combines shell efficiency $u$ and chemistry/opacity factors; $\gamma>1$ needed (requires $u>0.3$) for expansion beyond the ZAMS value $\gamma=1$.
- $L=4\pi R^2\sigma T_\text{eff}^4$ — Stefan-Boltzmann; explains constant-$L$, rising-$R$, falling-$T_\text{eff}$ on the SGB.
- $dT/dr \propto \kappa\rho L/(T^3 r^2)$, with $\kappa\propto(1+X)\rho^n$ — radiative gradient and opacity law used to derive $\gamma$.
- $L_H\propto\mu^7$ — extreme sensitivity of shell luminosity to mean molecular weight; explains the RGB Bump dip and the tight $M_c^\text{He}$–$L$ relation.
- $\log T=\tfrac23\log\rho+5$ — boundary between ideal-gas and non-relativistic degenerate electron gas.
- $P\propto\rho^{5/3}$ — degenerate electron pressure (T-independent), source of the runaway in the He Flash.
- $\varepsilon_{3\alpha}\propto T^{25}$ — extreme temperature sensitivity of the triple-alpha reaction, driving both the He Flash runaway and TP thermal pulses.
- $T/\rho^{2/3}>10^5$ — condition for degeneracy to be lifted during the He-flash cascade.
- $M_c^\text{He}\approx0.5\,M_\odot$ — universal He-core mass at ignition for all $M<2.2\,M_\odot$ stars; basis of the RGB Tip standard candle.
- $\log(L/L_\odot)\approx3.45$, $M_I\approx-4.0$ — universal RGB Tip luminosity, used as TRGB distance indicator: $(m-M)_I=m_I+4.0$.
- $g=GM/R^2$ and $\dot M\propto LR/M$ — surface gravity and general mass-loss scaling (low $g$ → high mass loss).
- Reimers law: $\dot M=-4\times10^{-13}\,\eta\,\dfrac{L}{gR}\;M_\odot\,\text{yr}^{-1}$ — empirical RGB mass-loss rate ($\eta\sim0.2$–0.5).
- $q=M_c/M_E$ — parameter setting ZAHB position; higher $q$ (thinner envelope) → hotter/bluer HB.
- $M_V(\text{ZAHB})\approx+0.5$ (metal-poor GCs) — ZAHB luminosity as a distance indicator.
- $\kappa_\text{FF}=D_\text{FF}(Z)/u^3$, $D_\text{FF}\propto Z^2$ — free-free opacity scaling; explains growing convective core during He-burning as C,O accumulate.
- $dP/P=4(s/r)\,d\rho/\rho$ and $(d\rho/\rho)(4s/r-\alpha)=\beta\,dT/T$ — thin-shell relations; when $4s/r<\alpha$, density decrease raises $T$, producing the TP-AGB thermal-pulse runaway.
- $t_\text{TP}\approx2\times10^5$ yr (0.6 $M_\odot$ CO core) vs. $\approx10^3$ yr (5 $M_\odot$ core) — inter-pulse timescale, shorter for more massive cores.
- s-process neutron capture: $A+n\to(A+1)+\gamma$, followed by $\beta$-decay if unstable — defines "slow" relative to decay time.
- $^{22}$Ne chain (needs $T\sim3.5\times10^8$K, $M>3\,M_\odot$): $^{14}N(\alpha,\gamma)^{18}F\to^{18}O(\alpha,\gamma)^{22}Ne(\alpha,n)^{25}Mg$ — high-mass neutron source.
- $^{13}$C source (needs $T\sim9\times10^7$K): $^{13}C(\alpha,n)^{16}O$, with $^{13}$C built via $^{12}C(p,\gamma)^{13}N(\beta^+)^{13}C$ — low-mass neutron source.
- $^{14}N(n,\gamma)^{15}N$ — neutron poison reaction suppressing the s-process unless $^{14}$N is converted away.
- Cameron-Fowler chain: $^3He(\alpha,\gamma)^7Be(e^-,\nu)^7Li$, destroyed by $^7Li(p,\alpha)^4He$ unless convected to cool layers.

## 3. Key Algorithms / Procedures

1. **Envelope-expansion derivation**: (i) equate $P,T,M$ at core–envelope boundary; (ii) get $\rho_i/\rho_e=\mu_i/\mu_e$ from the EoS; (iii) differentiate EoS to get the density-gradient ratio; (iv) get pressure-gradient ratio from hydrostatic equilibrium; (v) get temperature-gradient ratio from radiative transport + opacity law; (vi) combine into the master formula; (vii) evaluate $\gamma$ to determine if/how much the envelope must expand.
2. **He-Flash cascade**: Main Flash ignites off-center (due to neutrino-cooled temperature inversion) → convection lifts degeneracy in a shell above the ignition point → Second Flash ignites at new degenerate/non-degenerate boundary → cascade repeats inward until the whole core is non-degenerate (~$10^6$ yr, only ~5% of He converted) → post-flash core expansion/H-shell dimming (A→B) → stable 3$\alpha$ ignition at ZAHB (B→C).
3. **Convective-core evolution during He-burning (overshooting/semi-convection)**: Step 1 — overshooting mixes C,O outward and He inward, temporarily lowering $\nabla_\text{rad}$; Step 2 — $\nabla_\text{rad}$ develops a minimum that touches $\nabla_\text{ad}$, splitting the convective zone; Step 3 — the outer sub-region becomes marginally stable (semi-convective), yielding the three-zone (convective core / semi-convective / radiative) structure.
4. **HB blue loop**: Segment A — 3$\alpha$-driven core convection expands the core, pushes the H-shell outward/cooler, envelope contracts, star moves blueward; Segment B — core contraction as He depletes raises 3$\alpha$ rate, envelope expands, star moves redward; absent (no segment A) in EHB stars lacking an H-envelope.
5. **TP-AGB cycle**: H-shell burns and deposits He onto intershell → intershell mass reaches critical value → He-shell ignites (thermal pulse, runaway via thin-shell instability) → convection/expansion cools and switches off H-shell → He-shell fuel exhausts, intershell contracts → H-shell reignites → repeat.
6. **Third Dredge-Up**: (i) intershell convection during a thermal pulse homogenizes and C/O-enriches the intershell; (ii) after inner convection ceases, envelope convection deepens and reaches the enriched intershell; (iii) C,O-rich material is carried to the surface — repeated cycles raise surface C/O, eventually producing a Carbon Star.
7. **TRGB distance determination**: measure apparent $I$-band magnitude of the sharp RGB-tip discontinuity in the luminosity function → apply $(m-M)_I=m_I+4.0$ → correct for extinction and (weak) metallicity dependence → solve $5\log d-5=(m-M)_0$ for distance.

## 4. Watch Out For

- Both ingredients (chemical dichotomy **and** active shell, $u>0$) are required for expansion; dichotomy alone (Points 2→3) causes no significant expansion.
- $\gamma>1$ requires $u>0.3$ regardless of opacity law ($n=0$ or $1$) — don't assume any shell activity suffices.
- The Helium Flash releases $L\sim10^{11}L_\odot$ internally but produces **no observable brightening** at the surface — a common trap is assuming the flash is directly observed.
- The Main Flash is **off-center**, driven by neutrino cooling creating a temperature inversion — not a naive assumption that ignition starts at $r=0$.
- Only $\sim5\%$ of core He is converted during the entire flash cascade — the flashes remove degeneracy, they are not the main He-burning event.
- $M_c^\text{He}\approx0.5\,M_\odot$ is universal only for $M<2.2\,M_\odot$; stars below $0.5\,M_\odot$ never reach it and become He-WDs without HB.
- The TRGB standard candle applies **only** to populations older than ~1–2 Gyr (i.e., containing low-mass, degenerate-core stars) — not to young populations.
- HB position is set by $q=M_c/M_E$, not by total mass directly; since $M_c$ is fixed (~0.5 $M_\odot$), all variation comes from $M_E$ (mass loss + age + Y + metallicity).
- The "second parameter problem" is unresolved — do not attribute HB morphology to age and metallicity alone.
- Bolometric HB luminosity is nearly uniform across morphology types; apparent diversity in visual/UV bands is a bandpass effect, not a true luminosity spread (relevant to why EHB stars look faint in $R$-band).
- Overshooting/semi-convection only matters for the *core* during He-burning; during H-burning in massive MS stars the convective core *shrinks* (opposite trend) due to falling opacity as He replaces H.
- The Second Dredge-Up is significant only for $M>4$–$5\,M_\odot$ — do not conflate with the (universal) First Dredge-Up.
- The Third Dredge-Up requires two convective events that are **not simultaneous** — intershell convection first, then (separately) envelope convection reaching down; a common error is treating them as one mixing episode.
- $^{14}$N is a neutron poison: an efficient s-process needs conditions that remove it (He-rich, via the $^{22}$Ne chain) while independently generating $^{13}$C — the two neutron sources ($^{13}$C vs $^{22}$Ne) apply to different mass ranges and different temperatures.
- HBB explains why **massive** AGB stars ($M>6$–$7\,M_\odot$) do NOT become carbon stars despite active Third Dredge-Up — CNO cycling at the envelope base destroys the dredged-up $^{12}$C.
- Li production via Cameron-Fowler requires convective transport to cool layers **before** proton destruction — without convection, all Li is destroyed; this is why only bright LPVs (with deep envelope convection) show Li excess.
- Three critical masses must not be confused: $0.5\,M_\odot$ (He-ignition threshold), $2.2\,M_\odot$ (degenerate/non-degenerate He-core, RGB Ph-T), $8\,M_\odot$ (degenerate/non-degenerate CO-core, AGB Ph-T, WD vs. SN fate).
- $7$–$11\,M_\odot$ is a genuinely uncertain transition zone (O-Mg WD, electron-capture SN, or Type II SN) — not a clean boundary at exactly $8\,M_\odot$.
