# Final Stages of High-Mass Stars — Summary

## 1. Key Concepts

- **$M_\mathrm{up}$ ($\sim 8\,M_\odot$):** transition mass separating WD-forming stars from those that later form neutron stars/BHs.
- **Mildly degenerate CO core regime ($7$–$11\,M_\odot$):** ignites C under partial electron degeneracy → carbon flash → O-Ne WD or EC-SN.
- **No-degenerate CO core regime ($>11\,M_\odot$):** non-degenerate C ignition, full advanced-burning sequence to Type II CCSN.
- **Carbon flash:** He-flash analog; degeneracy decouples P from T so ignition runs away before expansion can cool it; occurs off-centre due to central neutrino cooling.
- **Super-AGB phase:** AGB-like evolution (2nd/3rd dredge-up, He-shell thermal pulses, dredge-out) around a compact O-Ne-Mg core instead of a CO core.
- **Electron-capture supernova (EC-SN):** collapse triggered by $e^-$ capture on Mg/Ne (not photodisintegration of Fe) when O-Ne-Mg core nears $M_\mathrm{Ch}$; weak explosion, low $^{56}$Ni, leaves a neutron star.
- **O-Ne white dwarf:** remnant of Super-AGB star if envelope is lost before core reaches $M_\mathrm{Ch}$; higher mass ($1.0$–$1.2\,M_\odot$) and faster cooling than CO WDs (larger mean ion mass → lower heat capacity).
- **Staircase structure:** alternating stable-burning plateaus and rapid gravitational-contraction ramps in $T$–time space, driven by neutrino losses forcing contraction between fuels.
- **Photo-disintegration:** high-energy photons breaking nuclei apart; drives Ne-burning, Si-burning, and (catastrophically) Fe-core breakup.
- **Nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE):** end-state of Si burning; abundance distribution set by nuclear binding energy, favoring iron-peak nuclei.
- **URCA process:** $p^+ + e^- \rightarrow n+\nu_e$, removes both electrons and energy with no compensating gain — pure energy sink.
- **Onion-shell structure:** concentric compositional layers (H/He/C/O/Ne/Si/Fe) built up by sequential burning, innermost = inert Fe core.
- **Adiabatic index $\Gamma$ instability:** collapse becomes unavoidable once $\Gamma<4/3$ (Jeans criterion violated).
- **Core bounce:** nuclear-density incompressibility halts inner-core infall and rebounds, launching the shock.
- **Neutrino-sphere:** surface below which neutrinos are trapped/diffusing, above which they escape freely.
- **Gain region:** shell between neutrino-sphere and stalled shock where neutrino absorption deposits energy.
- **Delayed neutrino-driven explosion (Wilson 1985):** currently favored mechanism reviving the stalled shock via neutrino heating in the gain region, ~100–500 ms post-bounce.
- **Explosive nucleosynthesis:** new, non-equilibrium nuclear burning triggered as the shock heats each onion shell during passage (distinct from pre-collapse hydrostatic burning).
- **r-process (rapid neutron capture):** neutron capture faster than beta decay; builds very neutron-rich nuclei up to heaviest elements; occurs in innermost ejecta. Contrast **s-process** (AGB, slow capture, follows valley of stability).
- **Recombination front:** boundary in the H envelope where H$^+$ recombines to HI, controlling opacity drop and hence the SN light curve peak/plateau.
- **SN spectroscopic classification:** empirical tree by H (Type I/II) → Si (Ia) → He (Ib/Ic); purely observational, not mechanism-based.
- **Prompt vs fallback black hole:** prompt = explosion fails outright (no visible SN); fallback = explosion succeeds but ejecta mass falls back, later pushing remnant over $M_\mathrm{NS,max}$.

## 2. Key Equations

- $^{12}\mathrm{C}+^4\mathrm{He}\rightarrow ^{16}\mathrm{O}+\gamma$ — converts C to O during/after He burning; makes CO (or ONe) cores O-rich.
- $^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{12}\mathrm{C}\rightarrow ^{20}\mathrm{Ne}+^4\mathrm{He}$ (mildly degenerate case) and $^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{12}\mathrm{C}\rightarrow ^{16}\mathrm{O}+2\,^4\mathrm{He}$ (non-degenerate case) — carbon-burning channels; both liberate $\alpha$ particles for further captures.
- $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}+\gamma\rightarrow ^{16}\mathrm{O}+^4\mathrm{He}$ then $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}+^4\mathrm{He}\rightarrow ^{24}\mathrm{Mg}+\gamma$ — neon burning; photodisintegration + alpha-capture, not direct fusion; occurs at lower T than O burning.
- $^{16}\mathrm{O}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\rightarrow ^{32}\mathrm{S}^*\rightarrow ^{28}\mathrm{Si}+^4\mathrm{He}$ (main), $\rightarrow ^{31}\mathrm{P}+p$, $\rightarrow ^{31}\mathrm{S}+n$ — oxygen burning; produces Si, S, P plus free n/p that build Ca, Ar.
- $^{28}\mathrm{Si}+\gamma\rightarrow ^{24}\mathrm{Mg}+^4\mathrm{He}$ ... down to $^{12}\mathrm{C}+\gamma\rightarrow 3\,^4\mathrm{He}$, then alpha-capture chain up to $^{52}\mathrm{Cr}+^4\mathrm{He}\rightarrow ^{56}\mathrm{Ni}+\gamma$ — Si burning is a photodisintegration cascade feeding an alpha-capture chain; ends in NSE at the iron peak.
- $^{56}\mathrm{Ni}\rightarrow ^{56}\mathrm{Co}(\tau_{1/2}=6.1\,\mathrm{d})\rightarrow ^{56}\mathrm{Fe}(\tau_{1/2}=77.1\,\mathrm{d})$ — radioactive decay chain; powers the SN light curve tail. Si burning yields Ni not Fe because weak interactions are too slow to equilibrate $n/p$ ratio in the available time.
- $^{56}\mathrm{Fe}+e^-\rightarrow ^{56}\mathrm{Mn}+\nu_e$ (and further captures) — electron capture on Fe-group nuclei; removes electron pressure support, triggers collapse.
- $^{56}\mathrm{Fe}+\gamma\rightarrow 13\,^4\mathrm{He}+4n$ (absorbs $\sim124\,\mathrm{MeV}$) — photodisintegration of Fe; endothermic, accelerates collapse and later saps the prompt shock ($\sim10^{51}\,\mathrm{erg}$ lost per $0.1\,M_\odot$ Fe traversed).
- $p^++e^-\rightarrow n+\nu_e$ — URCA process; net sink of both electrons and energy.
- $\tau_\mathrm{FF}\propto 1/\sqrt{\rho_0}$ — free-fall collapse timescale; gives $\tau_d\approx10\,\mathrm{ms}$ at $\rho\sim10^{10}\,\mathrm{g\,cm^{-3}}$.
- $\Delta E_g\approx GM^2/R_f\approx10^{53}\,\mathrm{erg}$ — total gravitational energy released in collapse (with $R_f\sim10\,\mathrm{km}$); only $\sim10^{51}\,\mathrm{erg}$ (1 Bethe) needed to unbind the envelope — the rest escapes as neutrinos.
- $\bar\nu_e+p^+\leftrightarrow n+e^+$, $\nu_e+n\leftrightarrow e^-+p^+$ — neutrino absorption reactions in the gain region that revive the stalled shock.
- $N(t)=N_0e^{-0.693t/\tau_{1/2}}$, $L\propto N(t)$, $d\ln L/dt=-0.693/\tau_{1/2}$, $dM_\mathrm{bol}/dt\propto 0.74/\tau_{1/2}$ — radioactive-tail decline law; the light-curve slope directly encodes the decaying isotope's half-life (universal $^{56}$Co slope = 77.1 d unless another isotope, e.g. $^{57}$Co, dominates later).
- r-process chain: ${}^A_ZX+n\rightarrow{}^{A+1}_ZX(\text{unstable})\rightarrow{}^{A+1}_{Z+1}X+e^-+\bar\nu_e$ — repeated fast captures build neutron-rich nuclei before beta decay; peaks at $A\approx80,130,195$ from neutron magic numbers $N=50,82,126$.

## 3. Key Algorithms / Procedures

1. **Determine a star's endpoint from He-core mass:** compare final He-core mass to thresholds ($<0.5\,M_\odot\to$ He-WD; up to $\sim7\,M_\odot\to$ CO-WD; $7$–$11\,M_\odot\to$ mildly degenerate C ignition; $>11\,M_\odot\to$ full advanced burning) to predict WD/EC-SN/CCSN fate.
2. **Carbon flash sequence (7–11 $M_\odot$):** (i) off-centre convective ignition where T peaks (degeneracy + central neutrino cooling) → (ii) convective flame propagates inward, erasing degeneracy → (iii) stable central C burning once flame reaches centre.
3. **Super-AGB → remnant decision:** track competition between carbon-shell mass deposition onto O-Ne core and envelope mass loss; if envelope lost first → O-Ne WD; if core reaches $M_\mathrm{Ch}$ first → EC-SN → neutron star.
4. **EC-SN collapse chain:** electron capture on Mg/Ne → loss of pressure support → runaway contraction (density↑ → Fermi energy↑ → more capture) → O ignition at $\rho\sim2.5\times10^{10}\,\mathrm{g\,cm^{-3}}$ as a deflagration → neutron star.
5. **Advanced burning staircase (>11 $M_\odot$):** sequentially ignite H→He→C→Ne→O→Si→Fe(inert); each stage: nuclear burning holds T roughly constant (horizontal step) until fuel exhausted, then neutrino losses force gravitational contraction (rising slope) that heats the core to the next ignition T; durations shrink from $10^7$ yr (H) to ~1 week (Si).
6. **Iron-core collapse sequence:** exhaustion of nuclear fuel → electron capture + photodisintegration + URCA all reduce pressure/remove energy → $\Gamma<4/3$ → free-fall collapse → core bounce at $\rho_\mathrm{nuc}$ → shock launch.
7. **Delayed explosion mechanism:** prompt shock stalls (loses energy to Fe photodisintegration + neutrino cooling behind shock) at ~100–200 km → neutrinos diffuse from neutrino-sphere into gain region → absorption deposits ~$10^{51}$ erg → shock revives and propagates outward (~100–500 ms post-bounce) → explosive nucleosynthesis in swept-up shells → ejection of everything outside the proto-neutron star.
8. **SN classification tree:** check for H lines (Type II vs I) → within Type I check Si lines (Ia) → within no-Si check He lines (Ib vs Ic).
9. **Light-curve phase progression (Type II):** shock breakout (UV/X-ray flash, T~200,000 K) → envelope expands/cools, opacity high (ionized H) → recombination front moves inward, opacity drops, trapped radiation escapes → optical peak → plateau (constant ~5,000 K recombination T, lasts until front clears H envelope) → radioactive tail powered by $^{56}$Co decay.

## 4. Watch Out For

- **7–11 $M_\odot$ carbon ignition is off-centre and a flash**, not central and stable — the opposite of what happens above $11\,M_\odot$. Lower mass within this range → more degenerate → flash occurs further off-centre.
- **EC-SN mechanism ≠ classical Fe-core collapse mechanism.** EC-SN is triggered by $e^-$ capture on Mg/Ne at $M_\mathrm{Ch}$, not by Fe photodisintegration; it produces weaker explosions and less $^{56}$Ni.
- **O-Ne WDs cool faster than CO WDs** despite being more massive — counter-intuitive; the cause is smaller ion number (larger mean $A$) giving lower heat capacity, not composition-specific opacity effects.
- **Neon burning happens at lower T than oxygen burning** even though Ne is heavier than O — because it proceeds by photodisintegration + alpha-capture, not direct heavy-ion fusion, so the ignition condition compares photon energy to nuclear binding, not Coulomb barrier height.
- **Silicon burning does not directly fuse two $^{28}$Si nuclei** — it is a photodisintegration cascade supplying alpha particles for a capture chain.
- **$^{56}$Ni, not $^{56}$Fe, is the direct product of Si burning** — a very common trap. Reason: weak interactions are too slow at Si-burning timescales to equilibrate $n/p$ ratio, so equal-N-Z $^{56}$Ni is favored over neutron-richer $^{56}$Fe.
- **Timescale collapse is driven by neutrino losses scaling as $T^9$, not by faster fusion rates** — the real reason each burning stage is drastically shorter than the last.
- **The iron produced in the pre-collapse core does NOT escape** — it stays locked in the compact remnant. All Fe seen in Type II ejecta comes from *explosive* nucleosynthesis of $^{56}$Ni in the shock, not from the hydrostatic Si-burning iron.
- **The prompt shock mechanism fails** — do not describe core collapse SNe as being powered directly by the bounce shock; it stalls and must be revived by delayed neutrino heating.
- **Only a small fraction (~1%) of the ~$10^{53}$ erg neutrino energy budget powers the explosion** — the coupling efficiency, not the total energy, is the bottleneck.
- **r-process vs s-process:** r-process nuclei lie far off the valley of stability (fast capture, slow beta decay catch-up); s-process nuclei stay on the valley of stability (slow capture allows beta decay between captures, occurs in AGB stars). Do not conflate sites or nuclide paths.
- **SN spectroscopic type is not the same as explosion mechanism** — Type Ia is thermonuclear (WD, no collapse, no remnant); Types II/Ib/Ic are all core-collapse, differing only by how much H/He envelope was stripped pre-explosion.
- **SNII-P vs SNII-L difference is envelope mass, not explosion energy** — IIL progenitors have lost most of the H envelope, cutting the plateau short/eliminating it.
- **Remnant mass thresholds ($M<25\,M_\odot\to$NS, $M>25\,M_\odot\to$BH) are approximate**, strongly modulated by the density gradient at the Fe/Si-O boundary (steep gradient favors successful explosion regardless of mass).
- **Prompt BH formation gives no visible supernova**; fallback BH formation *does* produce a (possibly dim/unusual) supernova — these are observationally distinguishable scenarios, don't treat "black hole formation" as always invisible.
- **Radioactive tail slope is universal (set by nuclear half-life)**, but the tail's amplitude/brightness depends on the (highly variable) amount of $^{56}$Ni synthesized — do not confuse "universal slope" with "universal brightness."
