# Supernovae & Neutron Stars — Summary

## 1. Key Concepts

- **Type II SN**: H present in spectrum; core collapse of massive star ($M>8$–$11\,M_\odot$).
- **Type Ia SN**: no H, Si present; thermonuclear disruption of a CO white dwarf at $M_{Ch}$; no remnant.
- **Type Ib SN**: no H, no Si, He present; core collapse of a star stripped of its H envelope.
- **Type Ic SN**: no H, no Si, no He; core collapse of a star stripped of both H and He envelopes.
- **Chandrasekhar mass ($M_{Ch}\approx1.4\,M_\odot$)**: maximum mass supportable by electron degeneracy pressure.
- **Double-degenerate scenario**: SN Ia progenitor route via merger of two CO-WDs driven by gravitational-wave orbital decay.
- **Single-degenerate scenario**: SN Ia progenitor route via accretion onto a CO-WD from a non-degenerate companion.
- **Nova phenomenon**: recurrent explosive H-shell burning on a WD at low $\dot M$; ejects most accreted mass, so it cannot build the WD to $M_{Ch}$.
- **Double-detonation mechanism**: He-shell detonation drives a secondary C detonation, destroying a sub-$M_{Ch}$ WD.
- **Deflagration**: subsonic thermonuclear burning front; allows expansion, yields mixed nucleosynthesis (W7 model).
- **Detonation**: supersonic burning front; incinerates unburned fuel at high density, would yield pure Fe-peak (ruled out alone by observed intermediate-mass lines).
- **Delayed detonation model**: deflagration transitions to detonation later; best match to observed SN Ia spectra/light curves.
- **Stretch-factor (Phillips) correction**: empirical width–luminosity relation making SN Ia "secondary" standard candles.
- **Electron-capture supernova (EC SN)**: collapse of a degenerate ONeMg core once $e^-$ captures on $^{20}$Ne/$^{24}$Mg push it past $M_{Ch}$ ($7$–$11\,M_\odot$ progenitors).
- **[el/H]**: logarithmic abundance ratio relative to solar; proxy for ISM metallicity at star's birth.
- **[$\alpha$/Fe]–[Fe/H] diagram**: chemical-clock diagnostic combining prompt ($\alpha$, SN II) and delayed (Fe, SN Ia) enrichment.
- **Plateau/Knee/Descending slope**: three regimes of the [$\alpha$/Fe]–[Fe/H] diagram, set by IMF (plateau height) and SFR (knee position).
- **s-process**: slow neutron capture in AGB (main, $A>90$) and massive stars (weak, $70<A<90$).
- **r-process**: rapid neutron capture producing heaviest/most neutron-rich nuclei; sites are CC SNe and kilonovae (confirmed via GW170817, 2017).
- **Neutronization**: suppression of $\beta^-$ decay in a fully degenerate electron sea, stabilizing neutron-rich nuclei.
- **Neutron drip**: density ($\sim4\times10^{11}$ g cm$^{-3}$) above which free neutrons exist outside nuclei.
- **Oppenheimer-Volkov limit**: maximum NS mass ($\sim2.5$–$3\,M_\odot$), poorly constrained (unknown super-nuclear EoS).
- **Pulsar**: rotating, magnetized NS emitting a beamed radiation lighthouse; discovered 1967 (Bell).
- **Characteristic (spindown) age** $\tau=P/\dot P$: order-of-magnitude pulsar age estimate; invalid for recycled pulsars.
- **Light cylinder**: radius where co-rotation speed would equal $c$; defines open vs. closed field lines.
- **Relativistic headlight effect**: beams isotropic emission into a narrow forward cone ($\theta\sim1/\gamma$) for relativistic sources.
- **Lighthouse effect**: misalignment of magnetic and rotation axes sweeps the beam past the observer once per rotation.
- **Dispersion measure (DM)**: column density of free electrons along the line of sight; used to estimate pulsar distance from frequency-dependent pulse delay.
- **Death line**: $P$–$\dot P$ boundary below which the pair-cascade mechanism cannot operate; pulsars beyond it enter the "graveyard."
- **Millisecond pulsar (MSP) / recycling scenario**: old, spun-down pulsar re-accelerated to ms periods by accretion from an evolving companion.
- **Alfvén (magnetospheric) radius**: where magnetic pressure balances the ram pressure of infalling gas, truncating the accretion disk.
- **Black Widow pulsar**: isolated MSP that has evaporated its low-mass companion via irradiation.
- **Bulge Fossil Fragment (BFF)**: massive bulge system (e.g. Terzan 5, Liller 1) with multiple stellar populations of different ages, proposed as a surviving primordial clump from hierarchical bulge assembly.

## 2. Key Equations

$$^{56}\text{Ni}\xrightarrow{\text{EC},\,t_{1/2}=6.1\text{d}}{}^{56}\text{Co}\xrightarrow{80\%\text{EC},20\%\beta^+,\,t_{1/2}=77\text{d}}{}^{56}\text{Fe}$$
Powers the SN Ia light curve (early rise/peak from Ni decay, late tail from Co decay).

$$\left[\frac{\text{el}}{\text{H}}\right]=\log\left(\frac{\text{el}}{\text{H}}\right)_*-\log\left(\frac{\text{el}}{\text{H}}\right)_\odot$$
Logarithmic metallicity/abundance scale, zero for the Sun.

$$M=\left(\frac{15.2\text{ km}}{R}\right)^3 M_\odot,\qquad R=14.6\left(\frac{10^{15}\text{ g cm}^{-3}}{\rho_c}\right)^{1/6}\text{km}$$
NS mass–radius and radius–central-density relations (degenerate neutron pressure support); more massive NS $\Rightarrow$ smaller/denser.

$$\frac{R_\text{core}^\text{pre-col}}{R_\text{core}^\text{post-col}}=\frac{m_n}{m_e}\left(\frac{Z}{A}\right)^{5/3}\approx500$$
Radius shrinkage factor from WD-like Fe core to NS during collapse.

$$P_{NS}=P_i\left(\frac{R_f}{R_i}\right)^2=4\times10^{-6}P_\text{pre-collapse}$$
Spin-up from angular momentum conservation during collapse (explains ms-period newborn NS).

$$B_{NS}=B_{WD}\left(\frac{R_{WD}}{R_{NS}}\right)^2$$
Magnetic flux conservation during collapse; amplifies modest WD fields to $10^{13}$–$10^{14}$ G.

$$\lambda T=0.29\text{ cm K};\qquad L=4\pi R^2\sigma T^4$$
Wien law and blackbody luminosity for NS thermal (X-ray) cooling emission.

$$\tau=\frac{P}{\dot P}$$
Pulsar characteristic (spindown) age.

$$K=\frac{1}{2}I\omega^2=\frac{2\pi^2I}{P^2},\qquad \frac{dK}{dt}=-\frac{4\pi^2I\dot P}{P^3}$$
Rotational kinetic energy and its loss rate; matches Crab Nebula's synchrotron luminosity, proving rotational powering.

$$R_\text{lc}=\frac{c}{\omega}=\frac{cP}{2\pi}$$
Light cylinder radius — boundary between closed/open magnetic field lines.

$$B^2=\frac{3Ic^3}{8\pi^2R^6}P\dot P\ \Rightarrow\ B\propto\sqrt{P\dot P}$$
Magnetic dipole spindown formula; constant-$B$ lines are diagonal in the $\log P$–$\log\dot P$ diagram.

$$P\approx2.42\times10^{-6}B^{1/2}\text{ s}$$
Approximate pulsar death line.

$$\Delta t\propto \text{DM}\left(\frac{1}{\nu_2^2}-\frac{1}{\nu_1^2}\right),\quad \text{DM}=\int n_e\,dl$$
Pulse dispersion delay vs. frequency; used to estimate pulsar distance.

$$r_A=\left(\frac{B_s^4R^{12}}{2GM\dot M^2}\right)^{1/7},\qquad r_d\approx0.5\,r_A$$
Alfvén (magnetospheric) radius and effective disk-disruption radius for accreting NS.

$$\frac{\dot P}{P}=-\frac{P}{2\pi I}\sqrt{\alpha}\left(\frac{B_s^2R^6G^3M^3\dot M^6}{2}\right)^{1/7}$$
Spin-up rate for accretion-powered NS; faster spin-up for higher $\dot M$, larger $B$, larger $M$.

## 3. Key Algorithms / Procedures

1. **SN classification tree**: check for H (Type II if present); else check Si (Ia); else check He (Ib) vs. no He (Ic).
2. **Single-degenerate SN Ia viability check (nova-rate diagram)**: locate $\dot M_H$ vs. $M_{WD}$ — low $\dot M$ → nova cycle (fails), high $\dot M$ → common envelope (fails), intermediate $\dot M$ → steady He-layer buildup toward $M_{Ch}$ (succeeds).
3. **W7/delayed-detonation nucleosynthesis logic**: burning starts as deflagration at $\rho\approx10^9$ g cm$^{-3}$ (inner $\sim0.85\,M_\odot$ → Fe-peak); WD expands, density drops in outer layers; transition to detonation (delayed-detonation) or continued deflagration (W7) produces intermediate-mass elements in outer ejecta.
4. **Mass–remnant mapping** (by ZAMS mass): $<0.08\,M_\odot$ → brown dwarf; $0.08$–$0.5$ → He-WD; $0.5$–$7$ → CO-WD; $7$–$11$ → ONe-WD or EC-SN; $11$–$25$ → NS; $>25$ → BH.
5. **NS internal structure traversal** (increasing $\rho$): outer crust (nuclei + degenerate $e^-$, electron capture) → inner crust (neutron drip, free neutrons appear) → interior (nuclear structures dissolve, $n{:}p{:}e\approx8{:}1{:}1$) → core (superfluid n, superconducting p; possible exotic matter).
6. **Recycling scenario for MSPs**: (1) NS born as young fast pulsar; (2) spins down toward graveyard; (3) companion evolves, Roche-lobe overflow spins NS up via accretion disk (X-ray binary phase); (4) NS emerges as MSP + He-WD companion; (5) appears in lower-left of $P$–$\dot P$ diagram.
7. **Black Widow evolution**: recycle NS to MSP → MSP wind irradiates/ablates low-mass companion → companion evaporated over Myr → isolated MSP remains.
8. **[$\alpha$/Fe]–[Fe/H] diagnostic reading**: identify plateau height (IMF), knee position (SFR — higher SFR shifts knee to higher [Fe/H]), and descending-slope shape to reconstruct a population's star-formation history.

## 4. Watch Out For

- Type I/II is purely spectroscopic (H presence); Ia vs. Ib/Ic is an explosion-mechanism distinction — Ia is thermonuclear, Ib/Ic/II are all core-collapse. Do not conflate spectroscopic Type I with a single physical mechanism.
- A pure detonation of a CO-WD is ruled out — it predicts only Fe-peak elements, contradicting observed Si/S/Ca lines. Only deflagration or delayed detonation reproduce real spectra.
- Sub-$M_{Ch}$ SN Ia (double-detonation via He-shell) is a valid but non-standard channel — do not assume all SN Ia reach $M_{Ch}$ by growth alone.
- SN Ia are only *secondary* standard candles — they need the stretch/Phillips width–luminosity correction before use as distance indicators.
- Type II SNe release ~300× more total energy than Type Ia, but ~99% escapes as neutrinos; optical luminosities of the two types are comparable despite this huge energy disparity — a common trap in energy-budget questions.
- NS masses cluster near $1.4\,M_\odot$ not by coincidence but because they descend from Fe cores supported by degenerate electrons near $M_{Ch}$.
- The Oppenheimer-Volkov limit, unlike $M_{Ch}$, is not precisely derived — it depends on an unknown super-nuclear equation of state (range $\sim2.0$–$2.7\,M_\odot$ across models).
- MSPs have short $P$ (young-like) but very small $\dot P$ (old-like) — this apparent paradox is resolved only by the recycling scenario, not by treating $\tau=P/\dot P$ at face value (that formula is invalid for recycled pulsars).
- Radio pulsar emission is powered by rotational kinetic energy, not thermal energy — thermal X-ray emission from an old NS is fainter than the Sun and generally undetectable directly.
- The narrow pulsar beam requires three ingredients together: relativistic beaming, magnetic/rotation axis misalignment, and fast rotation — missing any one gives no observed pulses.
- Distinguish the two roles of the [$\alpha$/Fe]–[Fe/H] diagram: plateau height ← IMF; knee position ← SFR. These are commonly swapped in exam answers.
- Iron-peak elements come mostly from Type Ia (2/3 of Galactic Fe) despite Type II being far more numerous per unit time early on; $\alpha$-elements come almost exclusively from Type II.
- r-process sites include both core-collapse SNe and neutron-star merger kilonovae — the kilonova contribution was only observationally confirmed in 2017 (GW170817), so don't treat it as settled decades-old physics.
- Bulge Fossil Fragments (Terzan 5, Liller 1) differ from normal globular clusters specifically by hosting multiple age/metallicity populations — a single old, coeval population is the globular-cluster norm, not the BFF signature.
